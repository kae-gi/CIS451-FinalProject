Hospital Management

CIS451, Fall 2020

Summary

The topic of interest is designing a basic model of a hospital management system. There are the included tables: patient, room, hospital, employee, scheduled hours, diagnosis results, bills, and tables describing nurse shifts and doctor shifts. More specific details about table attributes can be found in the Physical Design/Table Contents section. One such example would be a patient, who has describing attributes such as name, age, sex, and status, but who is also connected to where they are staying, how much they will be paying, as well as their diagnosis and who cared for them.

Applications included in this model are:
1a) calculating patient bill total
1b) what makes up that bill
2) given a hospital, show where patients are located
3) given a hospital, show when and where a nurse employee works and for how many hours for a single week
4) given a hospital, show when and where a doctor employee works and for how many hours for a single week
5) given a patient, show what their diagnosis is, status is, what hospital they are located, and what doctor diagnosed them

Physical Design/Table Contents

1. Bill table

describes each transaction (cost, description) for a patient's bill

2. Patient table

describes each patient, including name, age, sex, status, and the room_id they are staying

3. Employee table

describes each employee, including name, specialization (nurse/doctor), and hospital_id they are working

4. Hospital table

describes each hospital, including the name, address, city and state the hospital is located

5. Room table

describes each hospital room, including what wing it is located in (such as emergency room or surgery), the hospital_id, and the name of the room

6. Scheduled Hours Amount table

describes the amount of hours in a single generic week for each employee

7. Diagnosis Results table

describes the diagnosis results for each patient and the doctor that diagnosed the patient

8. Doctor Rounds table

describes a shift for doctors, including what time of day and where they are assigned

9. Nurse Rounds table

describes a shift for nurses, including what time of day and where they are assigned

Conclusion

If given more time, I would have liked to include more information in my tables, as well as gone more complex in my applications. More applications could have been included as well. Adding further customizability into the input for the applications would have been desirable. Improving the design of the pages in terms of aesthetics and functionality would have been a nice improvement.
